Found It! · The Life Game

A 54-Year-Old Liberal Arts Graduate's AI Experiment

I'm 54 years old, with a liberal arts background. Recently, I found myself with plenty of free time. Many of my peers chose to spend this phase earning income through physical labor, but I made a different decision — to invest my time in learning and using AI, trying to solve real problems with it.

This journey taught me something profound: AI is no longer a concept from science fiction. It's a tool that ordinary people can wield. A liberal arts graduate with zero coding experience can build an entire website from scratch with AI's help. The "Found It!" site you're looking at right now was born exactly this way.

Why "Found It!"?

While learning AI, I kept pondering one question: What's truly worth investing in long-term?

The answer gradually became clear — mental health for modern people is a long-term, systemic endeavor. Nearly everyone encounters psychological obstacles at some point in life, and we all have cognitive blind spots. These invisible barriers often cause more suffering than external difficulties.

So I took it further: What if we viewed life as a game?

Each of us is a player in this "Life Game." Some stages are largely determined by fate — the family you're born into, your social background. Others depend more on your own will and judgment — building your own family, forging your career. And some involve complex interactions — you influence others as they influence you.

Based on this idea, I designed a mini-game called "12 Stages of Life." By playing these games, people can quickly discover their cognitive barriers — what I call the "stuck points" — in a relaxed, enjoyable atmosphere.

Struggles Don't Have to Vanish — But Blind Spots Can Be Seen Now

I have to be honest: many of life's problems can't be solved immediately, even with clear understanding. Society is an incredibly complex system — beyond the mysterious "fate" that humans can never fully grasp, there are countless institutional designs that turn society into a giant noise generator, a vast web of relationships, a swamp that has humbled countless heroes. So many people chase happiness and success, only to fall short.

But on the other hand, society also offers a complex prism, a set of levers for amplifying value, virtually unlimited needs, and many possibilities for the future.

Struggles are shaped by social circumstances — they may not dissolve immediately;
but cognitive blind spots can be eliminated right away.

When you see more truth, problems stop tormenting you as much. This is the core belief behind "Found It!"

My Own Story

I've been through periods of pessimistic contemplation, ultimately choosing positive psychology as my direction. I've experienced nihilism, only to choose in the end to embrace and face real life head-on. I've accepted myself, and I've accepted my family.

Over the past year, I've spent a great deal of time with my child, helping her navigate the confusion and bewilderment of adolescence, working through psychological barriers. Now I appreciate my child more and more — just as I've learned to appreciate and accept myself.

There's much more to my story. As the site grows, you'll see more of my articles and gain deeper insight into what life has taught me.
