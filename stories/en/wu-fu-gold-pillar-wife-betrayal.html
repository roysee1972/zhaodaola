LEVEL 05 · LOVE & MARRIAGE

Wu Fu

—Gold in a Bridge Pillar

Life Principle
Do not blindly trust others; be cautious about trust in relationships. In solving cases, 'psychological insight combined with technical investigation' is an effective method for obtaining evidence. 'Entrusting one's heart to a wife, not knowing the wife has another in her heart' is a human weakness that requires vigilance.

⏳ The Setting

During the Ming Dynasty, Wu Fu (吴复) served as a local official responsible for handling various civil cases. The social and economic conditions of the time were complex, and life was difficult for common people. Marriages and neighborly relations were easily corroded by money. The case of Chen De (陈德) was a typical family tragedy triggered by poverty.

⚖ The Dilemma

Chen De had worked away from home for three years, saving up dozens of taels of gold. He hid the gold in a cavity of the third pillar of the Shuixinqiao (水心桥, Water-Heart Bridge). Upon returning home, he discovered his wife, née Lin (林氏), had been seduced by their neighbor Zhang Nu (张奴). When he went to retrieve his gold, he found it had already been stolen by Zhang Nu. He faced a dilemma: how to recover the gold while dealing with his wife's betrayal.

🎯 Motives

Wu Fu's goal was to help Chen De recover the gold and expose the crimes of Zhang Nu and Madam Lin. He employed the method of 'psychological insight combined with technical investigation.' Chen De's motive was to recover the gold, but his wife had betrayed him. Zhang Nu's motive was to steal the gold, and Madam Lin's motive was to maintain her affair with Zhang Nu.

🧩 The Framework

Wu Fu employed a cognitive framework of 'psychological insight' — he recognized that Chen De 'entrusted his heart to his wife, not knowing the wife had another in her heart,' meaning Chen De trusted his wife, but she already had another lover. He employed 'technical investigation' — sending a clever officer disguised as a beggar to spy on Madam Lin's home, obtaining evidence of Zhang Nu and Madam Lin's affair.

🧠 The Mindset

Wu Fu was shrewd and perceptive, seeing through human weakness — Chen De trusted his wife, but she had already betrayed him. He sneered at Chen De's naivety ('You entrusted your heart to your wife, not knowing your wife had another in her heart'), while using technical means to obtain evidence. Zhang Nu and Madam Lin thought they could escape unpunished, never expecting to be exposed.

💡 The Strategy

Wu Fu's strategy had three steps: first, interrogate Madam Lin to obtain her confession ('arrested Lin and interrogated her severely'); second, release Madam Lin to lower her guard ('released Lin to go home'); third, send a clever officer disguised as a beggar to spy on Madam Lin's home, obtaining evidence of Zhang Nu and Madam Lin's affair ('ordered a clever officer to disguise as a beggar and visit Lin's home to observe, obtaining records of Zhang and Lin's intimate exchanges').

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is 'psychological insight combined with technical investigation.' Wu Fu recognized that Chen De trusted his wife, but she had already betrayed him — this is a human weakness. He used technical means (sending an officer disguised as a beggar) to obtain evidence without alerting the suspects. Another model is 'lure to capture' — releasing Madam Lin to lower her guard, then secretly investigating.

🏛 The Outcome

Wu Fu successfully obtained evidence of Zhang Nu and Madam Lin's affair, and the gold was recovered. This case demonstrated the value of 'psychological insight combined with technical investigation' in solving cases — by understanding human weakness and using technical means to gather evidence. Wu Fu's criticism of Chen De ('You entrusted your heart to your wife, not knowing your wife had another in her heart') reflected a deep understanding of human nature.

💛 Why This Matters

Chen De's tragedy evokes sympathy — he worked away from home for three years to save gold, only to discover his wife had betrayed him. Wu Fu's wisdom is admirable — he saw through human weakness and used technical means to obtain evidence. The story teaches us: in relationships, trust is fragile and must be treated with caution.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is 'trust is the cornerstone of relationships, but trust can be betrayed' — Chen De trusted his wife, but she had already betrayed him. Through technical means, evidence of betrayed trust can be obtained. Extending to modern times: marriage investigations, corporate espionage, and cybersecurity all follow the same logic — using technical means to verify the authenticity of trust.
