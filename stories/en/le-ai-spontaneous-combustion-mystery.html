LEVEL 02 · LEARNING & EDUCATION

Le Ai

—Spontaneous Combustion Mystery

Life Principle
When facing a suspicious case, do not rush to suspect human foulplay — consider natural factors as well. Broad learning is an invaluable resource for solving problems — historical precedents contain wisdom for resolving present-day issues. "Hypothesis and verification" is the fundamental method for ensuring the reliability of conclusions.

⏳ The Setting

In ancient times, a government storehouse suddenly discovered that its stored oil-soaked silk (oiled fabric used for military or ceremonial equipment) had gone missing. The overseer (storehouse keeper) was suspected of stealing from his own stock or of gross negligence. Le Ai cited a historical precedent, pointing out that the oil-soaked silk may have been destroyed by spontaneous combustion of accumulated ash rather than stolen, thereby clearing the overseer's name.

⚖ The Dilemma

The disappearance of oil-soaked silk from the storehouse led to the most straightforward inference — it had been stolen, or the overseer himself had embezzled it. The overseer faced pressure to be held accountable, yet the actual fate of the silk was unknown. How could a reasonable explanation be found for a possibly innocent overseer when there was no direct evidence?

🎯 Motives

Le Ai's goal was to find a plausible "non-human" explanation for the overseer in order to avoid wrongly punishing an innocent person. His motivation came from his knowledge of historical precedents and his understanding of physical phenomena — oil-soaked silk is a flammable material that could spontaneously combust under certain conditions. Le Ai hoped to replace subjective speculation with scientific reasoning.

🧩 The Framework

Le Ai employed the cognitive framework of "historical analogy plus physical verification" — he cited the precedent of Zhang Hua during the reign of Emperor Wu of Jin ("The Jin armory caught fire, and Zhang Hua believed that ten thousand bolts of accumulated oil-soaked fabric would certainly burn"), pointing out that oil-soaked silk could spontaneously combust under certain conditions when stacked together. He then verified this hypothesis by checking whether accumulated ash (a residue of spontaneous combustion) could be found in the storehouse.

🧠 The Mindset

Le Ai was a man of profound learning. Faced with the suspicious disappearance of oil-soaked silk, he did not follow the crowd in suspecting the overseer but found an alternative explanation from historical precedents. He calmly proposed a hypothesis and recommended physical inspection to verify it. The overseer, thanks to Le Ai's help, avoided being wrongly condemned.

💡 The Strategy

Le Ai's strategy had two steps: first, cite the historical precedent to propose the hypothesis of "spontaneous combustion from accumulated ash" — "The Jin armory caught fire, and Zhang Hua believed that ten thousand bolts of accumulated oil-soaked fabric would certainly burn"; second, recommend checking the storehouse for accumulated ash — "If the storehouse contains ash, it is not the keeper's fault." Upon inspection, ash was indeed found, proving that the oil-soaked silk had been destroyed by spontaneous combustion and clearing the overseer of suspicion.

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is "historical experience guides present-day judgment" — by citing a similar historical event (the Jin armory fire), Le Ai provided a plausible explanation for the current case. Another model is the "hypothesis and verification" method — first proposing a hypothesis (spontaneous combustion from accumulated ash), then verifying it through physical inspection (finding ash), thus ensuring the reliability of the conclusion.

🏛 The Outcome

Upon inspecting the storehouse, ash was indeed found, proving that the oil-soaked silk had been destroyed by spontaneous combustion and clearing the overseer's name. This case demonstrated the value of "historical analogy plus physical verification" in judicial practice and highlighted the important role of broad learning in solving difficult problems.

💛 Why This Matters

Le Ai's profound learning and calm reasoning enabled an innocent person who might have been wrongly condemned to be cleared of suspicion — this wisdom of "saving others through knowledge" is deeply admirable. The story teaches us: when facing a suspicious case, do not rush to conclusions but consider all possibilities, especially natural factors.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "historical experience is an important resource for judging present problems" — events that occurred in history provide references and explanations for current issues. Extending to modern times: "literature reviews" in scientific research, "historical case comparisons" in accident investigations, and "historical data analysis" in risk assessment all follow the same logic — using historical experience to guide present-day judgment.
