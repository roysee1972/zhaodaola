陆文裕

— Expelling a Musician's Son from the Academy

Life Principle
The dignity of education outweighs the pride of the powerful. Uphold your principles without fear of authority. Better to have one fewer student than to let the academy be tainted.

⏳ The Setting

During the Ming Dynasty, Lu Wenyu served as the Provincial Education Commissioner of Shanxi, responsible for managing local schools. The Prince of Jin had a musician whom he greatly favored. The musician's son had been studying, and the previous deputy commissioner had examined and admitted him to the academy. Upon taking office, Lu Wenyu immediately issued an order to expel him.

⚖ The Dilemma

The musician's son favored by the Prince of Jin had been admitted to the school by the previous deputy commissioner. Lu Wenyu faced a dilemma: if he ignored it, the academy would be tainted; if he expelled the student, he would offend the Prince of Jin. This was a classic case of "powerful figures interfering in education" during the Ming Dynasty.

🎯 Motives

Lu Wenyu's goal was to preserve the integrity of the academy. His motivation was the dignity of education — "Better to have one fewer student than to let a single person taint the academy" — he would rather have fewer students than allow the academy to be sullied.

🧩 The Framework

Lu Wenyu adopted the cognitive framework of "educational purity." He believed the academy was a place for cultivating talent and must not be tainted by the selfish interests of the powerful. When the Prince of Jin repeatedly pleaded with him, he held firm and "resolutely refused to comply."

🧠 The Mindset

Lu Wenyu was upright and unbending, fearless before authority. He understood deeply that "better to have one fewer student than to let a single person taint the academy" — the dignity of education matters more than the pride of the powerful. This principled stance was the very foundation of his reputation as a great official.

💡 The Strategy

Step one: upon taking office, immediately issued an order to expel — taking action at once. Step two: when the Prince of Jin repeatedly pleaded, he "resolutely refused to comply." Step three: his justification was "better to have one fewer student than to let a single person taint the academy," defending the dignity of education.

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is "educational purity" — the academy is a place for cultivating talent and must not be tainted by the powerful. Another model is "rejecting power with reason" — using sound principles to refuse the unreasonable demands of the powerful.

🏛 The Outcome

Lu Wenyu expelled the musician's son from the academy, preserving its integrity. This demonstrated the value of "rejecting power with reason" in the field of education. Lu Wenyu's perseverance set a precedent for future educators.

💛 Why This Matters

Lu Wenyu's integrity is admirable — he feared not the Prince of Jin's power and upheld the dignity of education. His wisdom is striking — "better to have one fewer student" captures the essence of education in a single phrase. The story teaches us: the dignity of education outweighs the pride of the powerful.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "educational purity" — the academy is a place for cultivating talent and must not be tainted by the powerful. Extending to modern times: in the field of education, maintaining fairness and purity remains a core requirement.
