LEVEL 11 · HEALTH & AGING

曹克明

—以酋试药

Life Principle
Be vigilant toward unsolicited 'generosity' with unknown origins. If the other party truly believes in their own claims, they should be willing to bear the consequences themselves.

⏳ The Setting

During the Song Dynasty, Cao Keming (曹克明) was stationed at the frontier. A barbarian chieftain came to present a medicine, claiming it could heal arrow wounds instantly upon application. Cao Keming remained cautious about this medicine of unknown origin.

⚖ The Dilemma

The barbarian chieftain offered a medicine claiming miraculous effects. Refusing it might damage relations, but accepting it could mean falling into a poison trap. How could he verify the medicine's authenticity without causing offense?

🎯 Motives

Cao Keming's goal was to verify the medicine's authenticity while intimidating the barbarians. The chieftain's goal may have been to kill the Song commander with poison, or to gain trust with a fake remedy.

🧩 The Framework

Cao Keming proposed 'testing it on a real person' - he took an arrow and stabbed it into the chieftain's thigh, then applied the medicine. If the medicine truly worked, the chieftain would heal; if it was poison, the chieftain himself would bear the consequences.

🧠 The Mindset

Cao Keming was decisive and ruthless, giving the opponent no room for excuses. The chieftain had intended to harm others but never expected to become the test subject himself, and was terrified.

💡 The Strategy

Cao Keming turned the tables: since you claim the medicine works, prove it on yourself. This move both verified the medicine's authenticity and intimidated the barbarians.

🔬 The Mental Model

Cao Keming employed the strategy of 'using the opponent's own method against them': making the person who offered the medicine become its test subject - the most direct and effective form of verification.

🏛 The Outcome

The chieftain died immediately after the medicine was applied, proving it was poison. The barbarians left in shame and fear, never again daring to deceive Song commanders.

💛 Why This Matters

Facing a potential poison conspiracy, Cao Keming's decisiveness and wisdom are admirable. The chieftain's fate serves as a warning that those who seek to harm others ultimately bring harm upon themselves.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The core logic is 'making the claimant bear the consequences.' When someone promises you a certain effect, the best way to verify it is to have them bear the consequences themselves.
