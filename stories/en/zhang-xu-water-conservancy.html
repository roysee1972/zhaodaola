LEVEL 04 · INDEPENDENCE & SURVIVAL

Zhang Xu

—Water Conservancy and Census

Life Principle
No matter how large a problem, break it down into quantifiable tasks assigned to each person, and it can be solved quickly. Organizational ability is the rarest form of leadership.

⏳ The Setting

In Ming dynasty local governance, neglected water infrastructure and lax population management were chronic ailments. In Yunzhou, old irrigation channels had been silted up for decades with no one managing them. In Bazhou, residents roamed about without settling into productive occupations.

⚖ The Dilemma

In Yunzhou, silted-up canals left hundreds of thousands of acres of fertile land lying fallow. In Bazhou, large numbers of drifters and unclear household registrations meant tax revenue was nowhere to be found.

🎯 Motives

Zhang Xu (张需) made restoring water infrastructure and organizing household registration his core objectives, striving to help people settle down, prosper, and become reliable sources of tax revenue.

🧩 The Framework

Zhang Xu understood that "to govern the people, first govern their affairs" — working canals meant cultivable fields; clear household rolls meant manageable citizens. He used concrete, actionable projects and systems to solve abstract governance challenges.

🧠 The Mindset

Pragmatic and efficient, with exceptional drive for action, he did not rely on empty rhetoric but let tangible results win the people's hearts.

💡 The Strategy

In Yunzhou, he assembled the people to measure the channels, allocated a specific number of chi (feet) to each person, had everyone bring their own tools, and construction was completed in three days. In Bazhou, he set up a register for every li (neighborhood), recording every household's male and female members of all ages, leaving drifters nowhere to hide.

🔬 The Mental Model

The quantified allocation model: break a massive project into specific, measurable tasks per person (assigned lengths in chi), spark competitive motivation (each striving to outdo the other), and accomplish what seemed impossible in record time.

🏛 The Outcome

Yunzhou's water infrastructure was restored in just three days, and hundreds of thousands of acres of abandoned farmland were brought back under cultivation. After the household census in Bazhou, drifters returned to productive work, and tax coffers were replenished.

💛 Why This Matters

Accomplishing in three days what decades of bureaucracy had left undone — this is a powerful rebuttal to "bureaucratic inefficiency," proving that good leadership can work miracles.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

This embodies the management principles of "work breakdown" and "goal quantification": transform a vague grand objective into small, executable tasks for each person, and efficiency multiplies.
