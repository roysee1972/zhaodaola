LEVEL 11 · HEALTH & AGING

尹见心

—灯火验首辨奸谋

Life Principle
Narratives can be fabricated, but physical traces cannot be forged. When facing complex deceptions, returning to the most basic factual observation is often the most effective approach. Gently approaching the truth is more reliable than violently extracting lies.

⏳ The Setting

In ancient times, in a certain region, a wealthy man's nephew was murdered in the home. The wealthy man's eldest son had long been at odds with his wife. Seizing the chaos, he entered the room with a blade, severed his wife's head, and also removed the nephew's head. He reported both deaths to the authorities, attempting to create the false impression of 'killing in the act of adultery' to rid himself of the wife he despised. Yin Jianxin (尹见心) was assigned to investigate the case and needed to see through the conspiracy within this double murder.

⚖ The Dilemma

On the surface, the case appeared to be an 'adultery murder' - the nephew was killed, the eldest son's wife was beheaded, and the son claimed his wife had been having an affair with the nephew, hence 'catching them in the act.' But numerous doubts existed: the nephew was strangled, not beheaded, so why was his head also removed? How did the condition of the two heads differ? How could one prove the eldest son was not 'catching adulterers' but committing premeditated murder?

🎯 Motives

Yin Jianxin's goal was to uncover the truth and distinguish between two separate killings: the nephew was strangled by the wealthy man while drunk, and the eldest son's wife was deliberately beheaded by her husband. The eldest son's motive was to exploit the chaos of the nephew's death, frame it as 'adultery,' and eliminate the wife he despised.

🧩 The Framework

Yin Jianxin employed a 'physical trace analysis' cognitive framework. Although both heads were severed, the timing, method, and tools differed, inevitably leaving different physical traces on the cross-sections. Under lamplight, he carefully observed and found that one head showed 'skin and muscle contraction' (freshly severed, muscles retracting), while the other did not (severed long after death, muscles already relaxed). This difference directly revealed the time gap between the two killings.

🧠 The Mindset

Yin Jianxin was meticulous and not deceived by the surface 'adultery murder' narrative. He noticed the physical differences between the two heads - a detail most people would overlook. The eldest son believed his scheme was flawless - by mixing the two heads together when reporting to the authorities, he hoped to prevent officials from distinguishing the true cause of death.

💡 The Strategy

Yin Jianxin first carefully examined the cross-sections of both heads under lamplight, discovering the critical difference. He then made offerings of fruit and food separately for each head (an ancillary forensic method in ancient times), and gently interrogated each person involved with 'kind and detailed questioning,' ultimately uncovering the truth: the nephew was strangled by the wealthy man while drunk, and the eldest son seized the opportunity to behead his wife and remove the nephew's head, attempting to frame it on 'adultery.'

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is the 'post-mortem timeline' - after death, muscle states change over time: when beheaded immediately after death, the severed surface shows muscle contraction; when beheaded long after death, muscles are already relaxed and do not contract. Yin Jianxin used this biological knowledge to accurately determine the time gap between the two killings. Another model is 'gentle interrogation' - using kind and detailed questioning instead of torture to extract more reliable confessions.

🏛 The Outcome

Yin Jianxin uncovered the truth: the nephew was strangled by the wealthy man first, and the eldest son beheaded his wife afterward. The eldest son had reported to authorities under the guise of 'killing for adultery,' but it was actually premeditated murder. Both crimes were prosecuted separately, and the true culprits received their due punishment. This case demonstrated the decisive role of physical trace analysis in complex cases.

💛 Why This Matters

An innocent wife despised by her husband was killed, and the murderer even attempted to use 'adultery' to tarnish her reputation - such cruelty and cunning are infuriating. The scene of Yin Jianxin gazing at two severed heads under lamplight and instantly grasping the truth is filled with the radiance of wisdom. Behind the 'kind and detailed questioning' lies respect for human nature and dedication to truth.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is that 'physical traces do not lie': no matter how cleverly crafted a narrative deception may be, it cannot alter the true state of the physical world. Extending to the modern era: forensic science, physical evidence science, digital forensics, and other fields are all built on the foundation that 'physical evidence supersedes testimony.'
