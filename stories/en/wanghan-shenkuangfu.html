王罕 (王罕)

Listen to the "Mad" Woman

Life Principle
Never dismiss someone's complaint just because they "seem abnormal." Patient listening is the best way to uncover the truth. Behind apparent madness, real injustice may be hiding. Justice is not merely a legal ruling — it is respect for every person.

⏳ The Setting

In Tanzhou, a woman had become mentally deranged (suffering from madness). She repeatedly came to the governor's yamen to file complaints, but her speech was incoherent and disorganized. Previous governors had scolded and driven her away each time. When Wang Han took office, he did not treat her roughly as his predecessors had. Instead, he listened patiently, and ultimately discovered real injustice hidden behind her condition.

⚖ The Dilemma

A mentally deranged woman filing complaints repeatedly — anyone would assume she was talking nonsense. The previous governors had driven her away for exactly this reason. But Wang Han suspected: why would a mentally deranged person keep coming back to file complaints? Could real injustice be hiding behind her "madness"?

💡 The Strategy

Wang Han summoned the woman before him and patiently, gently inquired about her experience. At first her speech was still confused, but with Wang Han's patient guidance, she gradually began to speak coherently: she had been a man's wife but bore no children; after her husband died, his concubine who had a son drove her out and seized all the property. Wang Han promptly tried the concubine and restored the woman's property. The woman's mental illness was cured as a result.

🏛 The Outcome

Wang Han tried the concubine who had seized the property and restored the woman's wealth and status. The woman's mental illness was cured as a result. This case demonstrates the enormous value of "patient listening" in judicial practice — a "madwoman" dismissed by everyone spoke the truth under Wang Han's patient attention.

💛 Why This Matters

A woman who had lost her husband, her property, and her dignity — dismissed by everyone as a "madwoman" and driven away roughly — the injustice and despair she endured is heartbreaking. Wang Han did not disdain her; instead, he listened patiently and gave her a final hope. When the truth came to light and her property was restored, her mental illness was cured — justice is the best medicine.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is that "repeated complaints always have a reason" — a person does not repeatedly do the same thing without cause, even if they appear "abnormal." Extended to the modern world: "patient reception" in petition work, "unconditional listening" in counseling, and "taking complaints seriously" in consumer affairs all follow this same logic.

Reflection

1 Wang Han's key virtue was patience. Think about someone in your life who seems unreasonable or incoherent. What might change if you simply listened longer, the way Wang Han did?
2 The woman's mental illness cleared up once her injustice was resolved. What does this tell you about the relationship between emotional wounds and "irrational" behavior?
3 The previous governors all dismissed the woman as a nuisance. How often do people in authority — managers, doctors, officials — dismiss someone's complaint because the person doesn't fit their idea of a credible source?
