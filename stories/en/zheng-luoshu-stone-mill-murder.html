LEVEL 03 · ENTERING THE WORLD

郑洛书

—石磨合尸破命案

Life Principle
Do not dismiss something as evidence just because it is "common" — the abnormal state of a common object is itself a clue. Every crime leaves traces; the key is having eyes to discover them. Wisdom lies in finding the extraordinary within the ordinary.

⏳ The Setting

A submerged body was discovered, pressed down by a millstone. Millstones were commonplace — nearly every household had one. How could this ordinary millstone provide leads to solve the case? Zheng Luoshu (郑洛书) was assigned to investigate, needing to trace the killer through what appeared to be an unremarkable murder weapon.

⚖ The Dilemma

The body had been sunk into the water, weighted down with a millstone to prevent it from floating. Millstones were extremely common in rural areas, making it impossible to identify the killer through the millstone alone. But Zheng Luoshu realized something crucial: millstones consist of two halves — an upper stone and a lower stone. If the other half matching this millstone could be found, its source could be determined, and the killer identified.

🎯 Motives

Zheng Luoshu's goal was to find the killer through millstone matching. The killer's motive was robbery and murder — the victim was a fortune-teller from Jiangxi, and the homeowner killed him for his money.

🧩 The Framework

Zheng Luoshu adopted a "physical evidence matching" cognitive framework — since millstones consist of two halves that must be used in pairs, finding the matching half for the one found on the body would reveal the millstone's origin. This was a case of "finding individuality within commonality."

🧠 The Mindset

Zheng Luoshu was adept at approaching from angles others overlooked. Where others saw a millstone and thought "common object, untraceable," he thought "millstones must come in pairs — a missing half is inherently abnormal." This reverse thinking was the key to solving the case.

💡 The Strategy

Zheng Luoshu sent men to investigate nearby villages one by one, searching for households whose millstone was missing a half. They quickly found a villager whose millstone lacked the upper stone. When the millstone from the body was fitted together with the villager's millstone, the two halves matched perfectly. Under questioning, the killer immediately confessed, detailing the murder of the fortune-teller from Jiangxi.

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is "pairing verification" — leveraging the property that items must be used in pairs, and tracking down the missing half to identify the source. Another model is "elimination narrowing" — though millstones are common, few households would have a "missing half," so systematic investigation quickly narrows the suspect pool.

🏛 The Outcome

Through millstone matching, Zheng Luoshu swiftly identified the killer. The homeowner confessed and faced justice under the weight of irrefutable evidence. The wrongful death of the Jiangxi fortune-teller was avenged. This case demonstrated the unique value of "physical evidence matching" in criminal investigation — even common objects, once their pairing relationship is established, can become ironclad evidence.

💛 Why This Matters

A wandering fortune-teller trying to make a living far from home, murdered by his host for his money — the tragic fate of society's most vulnerable is heartbreaking. Zheng Luoshu used "millstone matching" to bring justice for the dead, demonstrating the power of wisdom. Even common objects can become ironclad evidence; the truth never hides behind the ordinary.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "pairing is identity" — when one half of a paired object is missing, the other half becomes its "identity card." Extending to the modern world: phone IMEI matching, vehicle VIN code matching, and DNA comparison are all advanced forms of "pairing verification."
