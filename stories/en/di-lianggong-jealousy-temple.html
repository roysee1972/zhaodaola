LEVEL 05 · LOVE & MARRIAGE

Di Lianggong

—The Jealousy Temple

Life Principle
Do not let unfounded fears dictate your decisions. When facing superstition and rumors, the most powerful response is to let facts speak and prove it through action.

⏳ The Setting

During the reign of Empress Wu Zetian of the Tang Dynasty, Di Renjie (狄仁杰), who was ennobled as Duke of Liang (hence called Di Lianggong 狄梁公), was assigned to travel an official route and oversee provisions along the way. Superstition was widespread at the time, and the Jealous Woman's Shrine (Dùnǚ Cí 妒女祠) was one such example — legend held that women passing by would bring disaster, and many people took detours to avoid it.

⚖ The Dilemma

When Di Lianggong's procession passed the Jealous Woman's Shrine, the accompanying official Li Xuanchong feared the shrine's 'jealous' curse and requested a new road be built to bypass it. A detour would waste manpower and resources, but proceeding directly left the entourage fearful and uneasy. A decision was needed to break through the superstitious mindset.

🎯 Motives

Di Lianggong hoped to use authority and reason to dispel superstition, maintain the dignity of the emperor's procession, and save the cost of a detour. He wanted to prove through action that the so-called 'jealous woman' was nothing but nonsense.

🧩 The Framework

Di Lianggong's thinking was based on the framework of 'using the grand to overcome the petty': the emperor's procession had a thousand chariots and ten thousand horsemen, with the Wind God clearing the dust and the Rain God sprinkling the road — how could a tiny shrine pose any threat? He used the authority of the grand to override the petty superstition.

🧠 The Mindset

Di Lianggong was composed and confident, unmoved by superstition. He did not simply issue orders, but used logical reasoning to dissolve fear, displaying the calm and wisdom of a man in high authority.

💡 The Strategy

Di Lianggong directly rejected the detour plan, using the grand narrative of 'the emperor's progress' to dispel the superstitious fear. He took no special protective measures — he simply proceeded normally, proving through fact that all was well.

🔬 The Mental Model

The mental model was 'righteous energy suppresses evil energy': when a person or group possesses sufficient legitimacy and strength, so-called evil spirits naturally retreat. The essence of superstition is psychological fear, which can be overcome with confidence and authority.

🏛 The Outcome

Sure enough, the journey passed without incident, vindicating Di Lianggong's judgment. This not only saved expenses but also set an example of dispelling superstition among the entourage.

💛 Why This Matters

When facing fear and superstition, someone needs to step forward and break the silence with reason and courage. Di Lianggong's composure and confidence gave people strength, showing that righteous energy can overcome all illusions.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is that 'fear stems from the unknown and herd mentality.' When you possess sufficient authority and reason to clearly analyze the essence of things, you will not be swayed by unfounded fears. This applies equally to modern management.
