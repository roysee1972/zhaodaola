LEVEL 10 · SPIRIT & BELIEF

Fan Zhongyan

—Resolving the Drum Omen

Life Principle
When faced with something that seems frightening, first analyze it with reason, then defuse it with humor. Most "supernatural" phenomena are simply natural events misunderstood.

⏳ The Setting

During the Northern Song dynasty, Fan Zhongyan (Fàn Zhòngyān, 范仲淹) — posthumously honored as Duke Wenzheng — was visiting the countryside when he encountered a household where a drum had rolled by itself into the courtyard and was spinning ceaselessly. In that era, such a phenomenon was widely regarded as a sign of supernatural evil.

⚖ The Dilemma

A drum had rolled into the courtyard on its own and kept spinning in circles. Everyone was terrified, believing a demonic force was at work. If left unaddressed, the fear would spread through the neighborhood. If handled with harsh measures — burning the drum or summoning exorcists — it would only deepen superstition.

💡 The Strategy

Fan Zhongyan said to his son Fan Chunren (Fàn Chúnrén): "This drum hasn't been beaten in so long that when it saw an honored guest arrive, it came to the courtyard on its own to find a drumstick." He then ordered a mallet to be fashioned and struck the drum, which shattered immediately. With humor and decisive action, he resolved the matter perfectly.

🏛 The Outcome

The drum was smashed, the "haunting" vanished, and everyone's fear dissolved. Fan Zhongyan gently taught the crowd a lesson: the so-called "ghost" was nothing more than an old, dried-out drum.

💛 Why This Matters

In the face of fear, a person who calmly says "it's nothing to worry about" with a touch of humor wields a quiet power. Fan Zhongyan's composure put everyone at ease.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic: "Fear springs from the unknown; the way to dissolve fear is to make the unknown laughable." When you can face the "supernatural" with a smile, it ceases to be frightening at all.

Reflection

1 Fan Zhongyan used humor instead of fear or force. Think of a situation where people around you were frightened by something you understood rationally. Did you use humor? Could you have?
2 The drum rolled because it was old and dried out. What everyday phenomena do people around you attribute to bad luck, omens, or supernatural forces that might have simple physical explanations?
3 Fan Zhongyan was teaching his son as well as calming the crowd. What does this story suggest about the responsibility of parents and teachers to model calm rationality?
