LEVEL 09 · INTERESTS & PASSIONS

Zōng Wēimǐn

— Winning Over Guests with Wine and Women

Life Principle
Brute force is not always the best approach. Find where the other person's desires lie, and use the satisfaction of those desires to achieve your goals — often you can get twice the result with half the effort.

⏳ The Setting

During the Northern Song dynasty, Zōng Rǔlín (宗汝霖, also known as Zōng Zé 宗泽) served as magistrate of Wéndēng County. A fellow jìnshì graduate, Huáng Róng (黄荣), a professor from Qīngzhōu, was demoted for submitting a memorial to the throne. While passing through Wéndēng, he caught a severe cold and could not continue his journey, but the military escort (yájiào 牙校) was urgently pressing him to move on, refusing to allow any stop.

⚖ The Dilemma

Huáng Róng was too ill to travel, yet the escorting officer relentlessly demanded they press on, unwilling to be flexible. Zōng Rǔlín, as a local official, wanted to help, but directly asking the officer to relent would be futile — the man already had his orders and would be hard to persuade.

🎯 Motives

Zōng Rǔlín needed to ensure Huáng Róng got sufficient rest to recover, while avoiding any direct confrontation with the officer. He needed to find a way to make the officer voluntarily delay the journey.

🧩 The Framework

Zōng Rǔlín discerned two weaknesses in the officer: a love of wine and a fondness for women. By satisfying these desires, he could erode the officer's determination, turning the delay into the officer's own "voluntary choice."

🧠 The Mindset

Zōng Rǔlín responded calmly, avoiding direct confrontation with the officer. Instead, he targeted the man's human weaknesses, demonstrating an indirect but highly effective problem-solving approach.

💡 The Strategy

Zōng Rǔlín first arranged for Huáng Róng to stay at an official lodge and sent a physician to treat him. Simultaneously, he secretly arranged for local clerks to take turns inviting the officer to drink and enjoy entertainment, frequenting courtesans' establishments. The officer, fond of drink and women, became thoroughly absorbed and refused to leave. Only after Huáng Róng had recovered was the officer prodded back onto the road.

🔬 The Mental Model

"Control through desire" — rather than opposing someone's will, use their desires to divert their attention, so that in satisfying their private wants they unwittingly cooperate with your goals.

🏛 The Outcome

Huáng Róng received ample rest until he fully recovered. The officer remained completely unaware that he had been manipulated. Zōng Rǔlín accomplished the rescue without attracting the slightest suspicion.

💛 Why This Matters

Stepping forward to help a friend in trouble, yet doing so not through brute force but through wisdom, embodies the ancient ideal of being both loyal and strategic. It shows how true friendship combines compassion with cleverness.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

Willpower is the root of action, and willpower is most easily dissolved by desire. Rather than opposing someone's will, satisfying their desires can undermine their resolve. In modern management, "using perks to defuse discontent" follows a similar logic.

Reflection

2What strategy did the main character use, and what does it reveal about the power of patience and insight?
3How might the outcome have changed if the character had chosen a different path?
