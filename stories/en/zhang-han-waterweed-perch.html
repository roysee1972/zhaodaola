LEVEL 09 · INTERESTS & PASSIONS

Zhang Han

—Waterweed and Perch

Life Principle
Knowing when to leave is more important than knowing how to advance. In turbulent times, preserving yourself and your family is the greatest wisdom. Nostalgia and mountain forests are the best havens in a chaotic world.

⏳ The Setting

During the War of the Eight Princes in the Western Jin dynasty, Sīmǎ Jiǒng (司马冏), the Prince of Qí, dominated the court. Government was in chaos as the princes vied for power. Perceptive individuals had already foreseen that even greater catastrophe was imminent. Against this backdrop, several people chose different methods of self-preservation: some found pretexts to leave, some feigned madness, and some retreated into the mountains.

⚖ The Dilemma

Zhāng Hàn (张翰), Gù Róng (顾荣), and Yǔ Gǔn (庾衮) each faced the same question: under the Prince of Qí's domination, with turmoil about to erupt, how could they preserve themselves? They had to choose between loyalty to their posts and saving their own lives, finding a balance between society's expectations and their own judgment.

🎯 Motives

The three men shared a common goal: to preserve themselves and their families in the coming catastrophe. Zhāng Hàn resigned office citing homesickness, Gù Róng used heavy drinking as a cover to stay out of politics, and Yǔ Gǔn retreated directly into the mountains. Their motivation was identical, but they chose different means of realization.

🧩 The Framework

The three employed a framework of "situational assessment" — by observing the Prince of Qí's power-grabbing behavior and the political situation at court, they judged that catastrophe was inevitable. They translated this judgment into concrete self-preservation actions, adopting the strategy of "of the thirty-six stratagems, the best is to retreat."

🧠 The Mindset

Zhāng Hàn's mindset was the most carefree — using the autumn wind and thoughts of water bass and perch as a poetic way to complete his political withdrawal. Gù Róng's mindset was more patient — lowering his political profile by feigning obsession with drink and pleasure. Yǔ Gǔn's mindset was the most resolute — taking his wife and children directly into the mountains to completely sever ties with politics.

💡 The Strategy

When the autumn wind rose, Zhāng Hàn felt nostalgic for the water shield (chún 莼) and perch (lú 鲈) of his hometown, and resigned that very day — completing his political risk-avoidance with a reason full of poetic charm. Gù Róng deliberately drank all day and ignored political affairs, making himself appear completely harmless. Yǔ Gǔn fled with his wife and children into the Lúlǜ Mountains, disappearing entirely from public life.

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is "retreat as advance" — when you cannot change the situation, voluntary withdrawal is the wisest choice. Another model is "existence itself is risk" — in a political vortex, your very presence is a risk; reducing your visibility reduces that risk.

🏛 The Outcome

Yǔ Gǔn lamented, "The Jin dynasty is in decline — catastrophe is about to erupt." His prediction was quickly fulfilled — the War of the Eight Princes escalated relentlessly, ultimately leading to the fall of Western Jin and the invasion of the Five Barbarians. Most of those who stayed behind met with misfortune, while Zhāng Hàn, Yǔ Gǔn, and others preserved their lives and families through timely withdrawal.

💛 Why This Matters

Zhāng Hàn's "thoughts of perch and water shield" are steeped in poetry and nostalgia, wrapping a political risk-avoidance decision in a romantic homesickness story. This ability to transform weighty reality into weightless poetry is among the most moving wisdom of Chinese culture. Yǔ Gǔn's words — "catastrophe is about to erupt" — carry the sorrow of a prophet.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "a wise person recognizes the times" — in the face of unstoppable great forces, active adaptation is wiser than passive resistance. This logic does not encourage cowardice, but emphasizes decisive action after clear judgment. As the saying goes, "While the green hills last, there'll be wood to burn" — preserve yourself, and there will be chances to rebuild.
