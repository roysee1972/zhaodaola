LEVEL 01 · FAMILY OF ORIGIN

韦孝宽 李崇

—植槐置鼓

Life Principle
A good solution should get better with use, not older. Prioritize systems that are alive, self-sustaining, and self-maintaining.

⏳ The Setting

During the Northern Dynasties, traffic markers and public security were two major challenges in local governance. Earthen mounds used as road markers would collapse after rain and required constant repair, while the presence of bandits demanded a rapid response mechanism.

⚖ The Dilemma

Wei Xiaokuan faced the problem of earthen road markers frequently deteriorating with high repair costs; Li Chong faced the problem of bandits committing crimes in scattered locations with slow information transmission. Both were persistent ailments of grassroots governance.

🎯 Motives

Wei Xiaokuan hoped to find a maintenance-free alternative for road markers; Li Chong hoped to establish a rapid-response village-level anti-theft network.

🧩 The Framework

Wei Xiaokuan: replace lifeless earthen mounds with living trees — a one-time investment with permanent returns. Li Chong: use sound signals to build an information network, connecting scattered villages into one unified system.

🧠 The Mindset

Both officials pursued a "fix it once and for all" approach to governance, unwilling to fall into a passive cycle of repeated repairs.

💡 The Strategy

Wei Xiaokuan ordered that scholar trees be planted wherever earthen mounds should have been placed — eliminating the need for repairs while also aiding travelers, with the added benefit of shade once the trees matured. Li Chong ordered each village to erect a tower with a drum hung from it; wherever bandits struck, the drum was beaten frantically. Neighboring villages heard the drumbeats and used the number of strikes as a signal. The sound carried for a hundred li, and all villages mobilized men to guard the passes.

🔬 The Mental Model

Wei Xiaokuan: replace inorganic decay with organic growth — trees grow stronger over time, while earthen mounds deteriorate with use. Li Chong: distributed network thinking — each node serves as both receiver and transmitter, with information propagating through the grid via sound waves.

🏛 The Outcome

The tree-planting solution solved the road marker maintenance problem, and once the scholar trees grew into shade, they also served as road landmarks. The drum system established an effective village-level joint defense network, making it nearly impossible for bandits to succeed.

💛 Why This Matters

The image of scholar trees casting shade along both sides of a great road is reassuring; the vision of drumbeats echoing across a hundred li carries an ancient, primal sense of power.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

Replace manually-maintained systems with self-growing ones; replace centralized management with distributed networks. Modern smart city sensor networks and IoT alarm systems are upgraded versions of Li Chong's "drum system."
