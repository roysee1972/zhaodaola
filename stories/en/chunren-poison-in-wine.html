LEVEL 09 · INTERESTS & PASSIONS

Fan Chunren (范纯仁)

The Poison Was Not in the Turtle, but in the Wine

Life Principle
Truth does not automatically become established just because someone confesses. Only by digging into the details can you approach the facts. Common sense and logic are the most reliable tools of judgment — more powerful than torture. Do not be deceived by surface cooperation; true wisdom lies in asking "why."

⏳ The Setting

In the Song Dynasty, cases of concubines conspiring with lovers to murder their husbands were not uncommon. Fan Chunren (范纯仁), son of the famous Northern Song statesman Fan Zhongyan (范仲淹), was known for his fairness and integrity. In this case, a concubine conspired with a petty official to poison Song Dannian (宋儋年), a military official, and attempted to cover up the truth with false testimony.

⚖ The Dilemma

Song Dannian died suddenly. When Fan Chunren sent his attendants to examine the body, they found bleeding from the mouth and nose — clearly not a natural death. Investigation revealed the concubine's affair with the petty official, and both confessed to poisoning the turtle meat. But Fan Chunren had a doubt: how could a poisoned man finish an entire meal? This contradiction became the key to cracking the case.

💡 The Strategy

The concubine and petty official's strategy was to confess to a half-truth — they admitted to poisoning, but shifted the poison's vehicle from wine to turtle meat. Fan Chunren's strategy was to press the question: "At which round of serving was the meat eaten?" Through timeline reasoning, he uncovered the contradiction. The critical action was continuing the deep interrogation, ultimately revealing that the poison was in the wine, not the turtle meat.

🏛 The Outcome

It was ultimately determined that Song Dannian was poisoned through wine after returning drunk. The concubine and petty official's false testimony was exposed. This case became a model of judicial prudence in the Song Dynasty, demonstrating the method of not blindly trusting confessions and investigating details thoroughly. Fan Chunren's reputation became even more distinguished.

💛 Why This Matters

The story of Fan Chunren, a loyal minister's son, inheriting his father's integrity is deeply moving. The concubine and petty official's betrayal and murder are infuriating, while Fan Chunren's insistence on missing no detail is admirable. The moment truth prevails brings a reassuring sense of justice served.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "consistency verification between testimony and facts" — any confession must withstand scrutiny by common sense and logic. In modern terms, this interrogation method relates to "cognitive load theory": liars must maintain consistency of false information, and detailed questioning exposes contradictions. This case also embodies Occam's Razor — the simplest explanation (the poison was in the wine) is often closest to the truth.

Reflection

1 Have you ever accepted someone's confession or explanation at face value, only to discover later that the details didn't add up? What would it take to develop the habit of questioning even 'settled' conclusions?
2 Fan Chunren used everyday common sense — 'a poisoned man can't finish dinner' — to crack a murder case. What ordinary knowledge from your own life or work could be applied as a powerful investigative tool if you looked at it the right way?
3 The suspects tried to control the narrative by confessing to a partial truth. Have you ever encountered a situation where someone offered a convenient version of events that everyone accepted too quickly?
