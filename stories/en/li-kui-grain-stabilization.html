LEVEL 04 · INDEPENDENCE & SURVIVAL

Li Kui

—Grain Price Stabilization

Life Principle
Store up in times of plenty to prepare for times of need. Don't squander resources in good times — maintain a long-term vision and plan ahead.

⏳ The Setting

During the Warring States period, Li Kui (李悝), a leading Legalist thinker, implemented sweeping reforms in the state of Wei. Among them, the "Grain Stabilization Law" was a landmark economic policy. In an agricultural society where harvests fluctuated wildly and grain prices swung high and low, social stability hung in the balance.

⚖ The Dilemma

In an agricultural society, bumper harvests drove grain prices so low that farmers suffered, while famine years sent prices soaring and starved the people. How could a balance be struck between abundance and scarcity — protecting farmers' livelihoods while ensuring food supply?

🎯 Motives

Li Kui aimed to establish a grain reserve and regulatory mechanism: purchasing grain during surplus years (to prevent cheap prices from hurting farmers) and selling it during famine years (to prevent high prices from hurting the people) — achieving the principle of "taking from abundance to fill what is lacking."

🧩 The Framework

Li Kui's thinking rested on a framework of "counter-cyclical regulation": in surplus years, when grain was plentiful and cheap, the government would buy and store it; in famine years, when grain was scarce and expensive, the government would release reserves to stabilize prices. This was one of the earliest examples of macroeconomic regulation in history.

🧠 The Mindset

As a leading Legalist, Li Kui valued practical results and refused to be bound by tradition. He applied systematic thinking to solve the grain problem, demonstrating the foresight of a true strategist.

💡 The Strategy

Li Kui classified harvests into three grades: upper bumper, middle bumper, and lower bumper. For an upper bumper harvest, the government purchased three-quarters of the surplus; for a middle bumper, one-half; for a lower bumper, one-quarter. This ensured ample reserves during famine years to stabilize grain prices.

🔬 The Mental Model

The mental model of "reserve regulation": build strategic reserves by buying when prices are low and selling when prices are high, thereby smoothing market fluctuations. This was the embryonic form of modern central banking and grain reserve systems.

🏛 The Outcome

After the Grain Stabilization Law was implemented, "grain prices stayed reasonable and the people did not scatter." Wei's grain prices stabilized, society became peaceful, and the state's power grew.

💛 Why This Matters

The principle of "taking from abundance to fill what is lacking" carries a warmth to it — the government was not merely regulating the economy, but protecting every ordinary citizen.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "using time differentials to smooth volatility." Bumper and famine years never occur simultaneously, so reserves can serve as a buffer between them. This principle has broad applications in modern finance, energy, and food security.
