LEVEL 03 · ENTERING THE WORLD

Bian Langzhong

—Maid Escape Reveals Injustice

Life Principle
Do not be swayed by a single authoritative judgment — truth requires multiple verification. Systematically collecting and cross-referencing information is more reliable than intuition. Maintain the integrity of the evidence chain, no matter how long the process takes.

⏳ The Setting

In Kaifeng during the Song Dynasty, the wife of a butcher named Hu (胡某) went out to fetch water and never returned. Around the same time, a woman's body was found in a dry well in Anye Ward. Hu identified the body as not being his wife, but the woman's father insisted it was his daughter. Under pressure from this "relative's identification," the authorities imprisoned Hu. He was tortured into confessing. When Bian Langzhong (边郎中) reviewed the case file, he keenly sensed the injustice within.

⚖ The Dilemma

The case faced multiple predicaments: first, "relative's identification" carried enormous evidentiary weight in ancient times — the father's identification was nearly equivalent to a verdict; second, Hu had already been coerced into confessing, and overturning the case meant reversing a previous official's judgment; third, the true identity of the deceased was unknown, making it impossible to establish Hu's alibi. Bian Langzhong needed to find an entirely new breakthrough.

🎯 Motives

Bian Langzhong's goal was to uncover the truth and exonerate the innocent. He recognized the miscarriage of justice at a single glance of the case file, drawing on years of judicial experience and deep understanding of human nature. Hu's motive was simple — he genuinely did not recognize the body and could not bear false witness. The father's motive was desperate grief: eager to "avenge" his daughter, he had lost rational judgment in his sorrow.

🧩 The Framework

Bian Langzhong adopted a "cross-verification" cognitive framework: rather than directly challenging the reliability of the relative's identification, he sought the true identity of the deceased to indirectly prove Hu's innocence. He reframed the question from "who is this body" to "are there any missing persons in the city whose description matches the body," replacing subjective judgment with systematic investigation.

🧠 The Mindset

Bian Langzhong remained calm and objective, refusing to be swayed by the surface facts of "relative's identification" and "confession." He was deeply familiar with common patterns of wrongful convictions in judicial practice — when direct evidence is lacking, it is easiest to yield to authoritative identification and coerced confessions. His insistence on "not releasing the case until the missing wife is found" demonstrated his unwavering commitment to the truth.

💡 The Strategy

Bian Langzhong first reviewed the case file and identified discrepancies. He then ordered a comprehensive sweep of all missing-person notices and arrest warrants posted at the city gates. Among the mass of information, he found a notice about a "merchant's escaped maid" whose physical description closely matched the deceased. But Bian Langzhong did not close the case on this alone — he insisted on finding the living maid to fully prove Hu's innocence. Eventually, when a former Kaifeng official traveled to Mingzhou to take up a new post, Hu's wife was spotted by chance among the welcoming crowd, and the truth was revealed.

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is "information network search" — gathering scattered information sources across the city (gate notices, arrest warrants) and systematically comparing them against known conditions (the body's characteristics). Another model is "evidence chain integrity": physical resemblance alone is insufficient for a verdict; the living key witness must be found to complete the evidence chain.

🏛 The Outcome

Hu was released without charge. The body the father had identified was confirmed to be the escaped maid of a merchant surnamed Hu. The real Mrs. Hu was found in Mingzhou, and the case was completely resolved. This case revealed the limitations of "relative's identification" as evidence, and demonstrated the value of systematic information retrieval in judicial practice.

💛 Why This Matters

A butcher thrown into prison solely because of a body he did not recognize — the desperation of the innocent is heartbreaking. Bian Langzhong's professional instinct, recognizing the injustice at a single glance, is admirable, and his insistence on finding the living person before closing the case shows profound respect for life. The moment Mrs. Hu was discovered in Mingzhou, justice arrived late but never absent.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "truth exists in information networks": a single information source (relative's identification) may err, but cross-referencing multiple sources can converge on the truth. Extending to the modern world: big data's "full-sample search" and "multi-source information fusion" are precisely the technological evolution of this ancient wisdom.
