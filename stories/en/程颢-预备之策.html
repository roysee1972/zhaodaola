程颢

—预备之策

Life Principle
Success comes to those who prepare; failure to those who don't. Plan ahead, reduce uncertainty, and you seize the initiative.

⏳ The Setting

During the Northern Song dynasty, Hedong Circuit (in modern Shanxi) faced a shortfall in fiscal revenue. Whenever the government procured supplies, prices skyrocketed. Cheng Hao (程颢), the renowned scholar-official known as Master Mingdao, served there under the dual pressure of fiscal crisis and runaway prices.

⚖ The Dilemma

The government needed to procure supplies, but prices were artificially inflated during official purchases — the government wasted money, and the people suffered. How could procurement be completed without adding to the people's burden?

🎯 Motives

Cheng Hao sought a solution that would fulfill the government's procurement needs while controlling prices and protecting the people's interests. He aimed to address the root cause of price inflation.

🧩 The Framework

Cheng Hao's thinking was grounded in a framework of "advance planning": rather than scrambling at the last minute and paying inflated prices, it was better to have wealthy families prepare supplies in advance, using stable expectations to control prices.

🧠 The Mindset

As a master of Neo-Confucian philosophy, Cheng Hao valued practical governance results. He did not merely discourse on abstract principles, but used pragmatic methods to solve real-world problems.

💡 The Strategy

Cheng Hao estimated the government's supply needs in advance, had wealthy families prepare them ahead of time, and set fair prices. This way, wealthy families enjoyed stable profits, while villagers' expenses dropped by twenty to thirty percent.

🔬 The Mental Model

The mental model of "expectation management": by announcing demand and prices in advance, market participants gain stable expectations, preventing the price spikes caused by last-minute panic buying.

🏛 The Outcome

"Wealthy families did not lose their profit, while villagers' expenses were no more than twenty to thirty percent of before" — the rich earned a fair return, the people's costs dropped dramatically, and the government completed its procurement. A triple win.

💛 Why This Matters

A great Neo-Confucian scholar who didn't just philosophize, but used pragmatic methods to reduce costs for the people, ensure profits for the wealthy, and fulfill the government's mission — this kind of "triple win" wisdom is truly admirable.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is that "uncertainty is the root cause of price volatility." Reduce uncertainty, and you stabilize prices. This principle has broad applications in modern supply chain management and government procurement.
