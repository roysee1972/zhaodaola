LEVEL 01 · FAMILY OF ORIGIN

Cai Jing

—Asking His Mother's Wish

Life Principle
When others argue endlessly on your behalf, the best approach is to let the person who truly has the deciding power speak for themselves. Respecting the party's own choice is the fairest judgment.

⏳ The Setting

During the Song dynasty, a woman married into two families in succession, bearing a son from each. Both sons competed to support their mother, and the dispute was brought to court. Cai Jing presided over the case.

⚖ The Dilemma

Both sons were willing to support their mother, but she could only live in one household. The law could not determine which son had greater right to support her, as both had legitimate familial ties.

🎯 Motives

Cai Jing's goal was to find a simple and fair solution. Both sons' goal was to have their mother live with them.

🧩 The Framework

Cai Jing did not get entangled in legal technicalities or ethical hierarchies, but proposed the simplest solution: "What's difficult about this? Just ask the mother what she wants." — simply ask the mother where she wishes to live.

🧠 The Mindset

Cai Jing was relaxed and effortless, simplifying a complex problem. The two sons, meanwhile, each insisted on their own position and refused to yield.

💡 The Strategy

Cai Jing cut through the matter with one remark: since both want to support their mother, let the mother choose for herself. This was the solution that most respected the mother's wishes and the fairest possible judgment.

🔬 The Mental Model

Cai Jing applied the thinking of "returning to the party concerned": when a third party finds it difficult to judge, letting the person most concerned decide for themselves is the most reasonable approach. This avoids the subjective bias of the adjudicator.

🏛 The Outcome

Cai Jing's solution was adopted — the mother chose the son she wished to live with, and the dispute was resolved. This judgment demonstrated respect for the party's wishes.

💛 Why This Matters

Both sons competed to support their mother, demonstrating filial piety. Cai Jing's solution ensured the mother's wishes were respected, and both sons' devotion found its proper place.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The core logic is "respect the party's autonomy." In matters involving personal choice, the wishes of the person most concerned should be the most important consideration.
