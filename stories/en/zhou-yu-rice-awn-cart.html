LEVEL 03 · ENTERING THE WORLD

Zhou Yu

—Rice Awn on a Cart

Life Principle
Even the tiniest trace can become the key to solving a case. When facing intimidation and provocation, calm observation is more effective than angry reaction. Do not be deceived by surface horror — investigating "how it was done" matters more than focusing on "what was done."

⏳ The Setting

In ancient times, Zhou Yu (周纡) was renowned for his strict governance, which struck fear into local power brokers and petty officials. To undermine his authority, a clerk named Ting Yuan (廷掾) schemed to play a macabre prank: before dawn, he severed the limbs of a corpse and propped it at the temple gate, intending to create an atmosphere of terror to shake Zhou Yu's authority. When Zhou Yu arrived at the scene, he did not panic. Instead, he calmly "conversed with the dead man," secretly observing and discovering a crucial clue.

⚖ The Dilemma

Zhou Yu faced an intimidation gambit — someone had deliberately placed a mutilated corpse before him, attempting to frighten him and undermine his authority. If he showed weakness, he would lose control over his subordinates; if he pursued the case in fury, he might alert the culprit. He needed to quietly identify the mastermind without revealing his investigative intentions.

🎯 Motives

Zhou Yu's goal was to identify the mastermind who placed the corpse at the temple gate and maintain his authority. The clerk's motive was to weaken Zhou Yu's stern image through a terrorizing event, to show him "who's boss." Zhou Yu had to respond with calm composure and wisdom.

🧩 The Framework

Zhou Yu adopted a "physical trace tracing" cognitive framework — he disregarded the corpse itself (which was merely a prop for intimidation) and focused on the process of how it was transported to the temple gate. He found rice husk traces in the corpse's mouth and eyes, inferring it had been wrapped in straw for transport. By questioning the gatekeeper, he confirmed who had entered the city with a straw cart, thereby identifying the suspect.

🧠 The Mindset

Zhou Yu displayed extraordinary composure — he "appeared to converse with the corpse," pretending to talk to the dead man while actually examining the body's details up close. This unhurried demeanor both fooled the clerk who was secretly watching and bought Zhou Yu time for a thorough inspection. The clerk believed his prank had succeeded, never imagining Zhou Yu's response would be so utterly unexpected.

💡 The Strategy

Upon arriving at the scene, Zhou Yu pretended to converse with the dead man while secretly examining the corpse's mouth and eyes, discovering rice husk traces. He deduced the body had been wrapped in straw and transported to the temple gate. He then secretly questioned the gatekeeper: who entered the city today with a straw cart? The gatekeeper answered that only the clerk Ting Yuan had done so. Zhou Yu immediately arrested Ting Yuan, who confessed everything under interrogation.

🔬 The Mental Model

The core model is "transport trace chain" — any object moved from point A to point B leaves traces on the transport vehicle and along the route. The rice husks on the corpse proved it had been wrapped in straw, and straw-wrapped cargo required a vehicle for transport. By investigating "who entered the city with a straw cart today," Zhou Yu narrowed the investigation to a single suspect. Another model is "covert investigation" — Zhou Yu did not investigate openly, but observed and questioned in secret, avoiding alerting the culprit.

🏛 The Outcome

The clerk was arrested and confessed everything, admitting to the prank he had orchestrated to undermine Zhou Yu's authority. Far from being shaken, Zhou Yu became even more feared and respected for solving the case so swiftly. This case demonstrated the core value of "physical evidence tracking" in investigations.

💛 Why This Matters

Faced with the intimidation of severed limbs, Zhou Yu remained unperturbed and composed — such courage is admirable. An insignificant rice husk became the key to solving the case, illustrating the profound truth that "details determine success or failure." The clerk's clever "intimidation gambit" ultimately became his own undoing.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is "movement must leave traces": any spatial displacement of an object leaves traces on both the object and its transport vehicle. Extending to the modern world: metadata analysis in digital forensics and supply chain auditing in logistics tracking follow the same principle — tracing the chain of evidence is the most reliable method of reconstructing the truth.
