LEVEL 04 · INDEPENDENCE & SURVIVAL

Huang Zhen

—Another Way to Stabilize Prices

Life Principle
When setting rules, apply mutual constraints — preventing one side from becoming too powerful while keeping the other from resorting to desperate measures.

⏳ The Setting

During the Southern Song dynasty, Huang Zhen (黄震) served as a local official when famine struck and rice prices soared. Unlike Zhao Qingxian's strategy of "raising prices to attract merchants," Huang Zhen adopted a balanced approach combining firmness and flexibility to stabilize rice prices.

⚖ The Dilemma

During the famine, rice prices skyrocketed. Wealthy households hoarded grain and refused to sell; the poor had nothing to eat and might resort to desperate robbery. How could property rights be protected while still getting grain to flow into the market?

🎯 Motives

Huang Zhen needed a solution that both protected wealthy households' interests and ensured grain circulation. He could not simply force the rich to sell — that would violate property rights. But neither could he do nothing, as that would spark social unrest.

🧩 The Framework

Huang Zhen's thinking was grounded in a framework of "clear rules": as long as rules were unambiguous and firmly enforced, people would comply. He used just eight characters — "Hoarders shall be registered; robbers shall be executed" — to constrain both the wealthy and the poor simultaneously.

🧠 The Mindset

Huang Zhen was upright yet flexible. He did not force the rich to sell grain, but used the threat of "registration" to pressure them. He did not condone robbery by the poor, but used the threat of execution to deter them.

💡 The Strategy

Huang Zhen assembled the wealthy families and announced just eight characters: "Hoarders shall be registered; robbers shall be executed." Those who hoarded grain without selling would be registered (and potentially punished); those who forcibly seized grain would face execution. These eight characters constrained both sides at once.

🔬 The Mental Model

The mental model of "mutual constraint": set rules for both supply and demand sides simultaneously, preventing both hoarding and violent seizures, using rules to maintain market order.

🏛 The Outcome

"Rice prices thus stabilized" — once the rules were clear, the wealthy dared not hoard, the poor dared not rob, grain flowed normally, and prices naturally declined.

💛 Why This Matters

Eight characters — concise and powerful — that protected the weak and maintained order alike. This wisdom of "moving a thousand pounds with four ounces" commands deep admiration.

🔮 The Deeper Lesson

The underlying logic is that "rules are more effective than orders." Orders are one-directional and easily provoke resistance; rules are bidirectional, giving everyone clear guidelines to follow.
